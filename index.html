Hackthons

2025 Live AI: TurkEye --- AI-Powered Content Moderation System

TurkEye is a multimodal AI system designed to detect and explain deepfake images through a combination of visual and textual analysis. The system integrates a ConvNeXt backbone for high-resolution feature extraction with a domain-adversarial neural network (DANN) to promote generator-invariant representations. To enhance interpretability, TurkEye employs a captioning module that generates natural language explanations of manipulated regions, alongside a visual saliency map highlighting tampered areas. This architecture enables TurkEye to provide both accurate classification and human-understandable explanations, facilitating trust and transparency in deepfake detection.

2025 Harvard University Rare Disease Hackathon: RareMind --- Web portal that helps patients keep track of all their medical data

The RareMind project is a patient-centric web portal designed to assist individuals with rare diseases in managing their medical data and obtaining personalized diagnostic insights. It offers multiple methods for identifying Human Phenotype Ontology (HPO) terms: direct entry of known HPO codes, extraction from uploaded clinical notes using ClinPhen, and an interactive chatbot powered by a large language model to interpret symptoms described in layman's terms. The platform is built using a FastAPI backend and a React frontend, orchestrated with Docker for seamless deployment. This integrated approach empowers patients to refine their symptom profiles, receive differential diagnoses, and generate comprehensive reports to facilitate discussions with healthcare providers.

  • Find out more on GitHub

2023 ASA DataFest

Our team analyzed legal conversations from ABA’s pro bono platform to uncover what makes a question more likely to receive an answer. We used BERT embeddings and TF-IDF features to identify semantic and structural patterns, then applied SHAP to highlight predictive linguistic cues. By combining these features in a logistic regression model, we achieved 87% accuracy in classifying answerable legal queries.
